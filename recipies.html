Dry Roasted Crickets

You will need:

As many crickets as you wish and a seasoning such as salt

You Should:

  1. Preheat your oven to 200 degrees. Arrange the crickets on a baking tray, so that none of them overlap.
  2. Proceed to bake at low temperature for about 45 minutes/until the crickets are completely dry (the crickets should be easily crushed)
  3. Remove wings and the antennae
  4. Season them with your chosen seasoning!

Once you have finished, either enjoy your snack, or freeze your crickets for later.

Chocolate Covered Crickets

You will need:

  • 1 unit of roasted crickets (note the above recipe)
  • 1 unit of chocolate (to be melted)

You Should:

  1. Melt the chocolate (the methodology for this will vary depending on the type of chocolate)
  2. Place the roasted crickets in the melted chocolate
  3. Scoop the crickets out, making sure they are covered in chocolate
  4. Place them on wax paper and let them freeze for an hour or two.

Chocolate Chirp Cookies

You will need:

  • 350g of unsifted flour.
  • 0.5 cups of dry-roasted crickets (note the above recipe).
  • 1 tsp of baking soda.
  • 1 tsp of salt.
  • 1 tsp of vanilla extract.
  • 225g of butter.
  • 175g of sugar.
  • 175g of brown sugar
  • 350g of Chocolate Chips.
  • 2 eggs.

You Should:

  1. Preheat oven to 190 deg C
  2. Melt Chocolate
  3. Dip dry-roasted crickets into chocolate
  4. Let them dry on a plate
  5. Mix flour, baking soda and salt in one bowl
  6. Mix the butter, sugar (white and brown) and vanilla in the other bowl
  7. Beat your second bowl until it is cream
  8. Beat the eggs into your second bowl and then gradually add the flour mixture from your first bowl
  9. Add the chocolate covered crickets
  10. Add teaspoons of the mixture onto a cookie sheet
  11. Bake for 8-10 minutes
